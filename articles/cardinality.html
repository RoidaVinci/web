Cardinality Exercises

Global Exercise

Study the cardinality of the following sets in relation to those of \( \mathbb{N}, \mathbb{R}, \mathcal{P}(\mathbb{R}) \).

Exercise 1: \( \mathbb{Z} \)

Prove that the set of integers \( \mathbb{Z} \) has the same cardinality as \( \mathbb{N} \).

Solution:

The function:

\( f:\mathbb{Z} \rightarrow \mathbb{N} \)
\( n \mapsto f(n) = \begin{cases} 2n & \text{if } n \geq 0 \\ -(2n-1) & \text{if } n < 0 \end{cases} \)

is a bijection. Hence, \( |\mathbb{Z}| = |\mathbb{N}| \).

Exercise 2: \( \mathbb{N} \times \mathbb{N} \)

Prove that the Cartesian product of natural numbers \( \mathbb{N} \times \mathbb{N} \) has the same cardinality as \( \mathbb{N} \).

Solution:

The functions:

\( \theta:\mathbb{N} \times \mathbb{N} \rightarrow \mathbb{N} \)
\( (p, q) \mapsto \theta(p, q) = 2^p \cdot 3^q \)

\( i:\mathbb{N} \rightarrow \mathbb{Z} \times \mathbb{N} \)
\( n \mapsto i(n) = (n, n) \)

are injections between \( \mathbb{N} \times \mathbb{N} \) and \( \mathbb{N} \), and vice versa. Therefore, \( |\mathbb{N} \times \mathbb{N}| = |\mathbb{N}| \).

Exercise 3: \( \mathbb{Q} \)

Prove that the set of rational numbers \( \mathbb{Q} \) has the same cardinality as \( \mathbb{N} \).

Solution:

To ensure the following function is well-defined, let \( \frac{p}{q} \in \mathbb{Q} \) with \( p \in \mathbb{Z} \) and \( q \in \mathbb{N} \setminus \{0\} \) be an irreducible fraction uniquely characterizing a rational number. Then:

\( f:\mathbb{Q} \rightarrow \mathbb{N} \)
\( \frac{p}{q} \mapsto f\left(\frac{p}{q}\right) = \begin{cases} 2^p \cdot 3^q & \text{if } p \geq 0 \\ 5^{-p} \cdot 3^q & \text{if } p < 0 \end{cases} \)

is an injection. Another injection:

\( i:\mathbb{N} \rightarrow \mathbb{Q} \)
\( n \mapsto i(n) = n \in \mathbb{Q} \)

is also an injection. Therefore, \( |\mathbb{Q}| = |\mathbb{N}| \).
