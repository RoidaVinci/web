Mathematical Exercises

Exercise 1: Let \( X := \left\lbrace \sum_{k\in U}2^{-k} : U\subset 2\mathbb{N}-1\right\rbrace \).

Observation: If \( x \in X \) and \( x = 0.x_1x_2x_3\dots_{(2)} = \sum_{n=0}^\infty x_n \cdot 2^{-k} \) with \( x_n \in \{0,1\} \) for all \( n \in \mathbb{N} \) is the binary expression of \( x \), then by the definition of \( X \), we have \( x_{2k} = 0 \) for all \( k \in \mathbb{N} \), that is, the even positions in the binary expression of \( x \) are all 0.

  1. Prove that there is a bijection between \( X \) and \( \mathbb{R} \).
  2. Give an explicit bijection between \( X \) and \( [0,1) \).
  3. Prove that if \( x, y \in X \) and \( x \neq y \), then \( \frac{x+y}{2} \not\in X \).

Solutions:

Solution to Exercise 1:

Solution 1: We will directly solve the second part since it immediately proves the first. To do this, we define the function:

$$ \kappa: U \in \mathcal{P}(2\mathbb{N}-1) \longmapsto \kappa(U) = \left\{\dfrac{n+1}{2}: n \in U\right\} \in \mathcal{P}(\mathbb{N}) $$

It is evident that \( \kappa \) is a bijection between \( \mathcal{P}(2\mathbb{N}-1) \) and \( \mathcal{P}(\mathbb{N}) \). Therefore, it follows clearly that the following function is a bijection:

$$ \xi: x = \sum_{k \in U} 2^{-k} \in X \longmapsto \xi(x) = \sum_{k \in \kappa(U)} 2^{-k} \in \left\lbrace \sum_{k \in V} 2^{-k} : V \subset \mathbb{N} \right\rbrace := Y $$

Since \( Y \) is the set of all binary expressions of the numbers in the interval \( [0,1) \), it follows that \( Y = [0,1) \) and therefore \( \xi \) is the bijection sought.

Solution 2: Let \( x, y \in X, x \neq y \); without loss of generality, assume \( x \) has a 1 in a position (odd) where \( y \) has a 0 (otherwise all their digits would be the same and \( x = y \)). When summing them in base 2, that 1 remains in the same odd position, and when divided by two, it moves to the next position, which is even, and thus \( \frac{x+y}{2} \not\in X \).
